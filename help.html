Getting Help During Climate‑Related Events

People living in poverty are often the most affected by floods, storms, heatwaves, and other climate‑related disasters. These resources offer direct support during emergencies:

  • Local emergency shelters — safe places to stay during storms or extreme heat.
  • Community relief centers — provide food, water, and essential supplies.
  • Disaster assistance programs — help with evacuation, recovery, and rebuilding.
  • Social service agencies — offer financial support and long‑term stability programs.

Reaching out early can reduce harm and help families recover faster.

How We Can Help Reduce Risk

SDG Target 1.5 focuses on protecting vulnerable communities by strengthening resilience. These actions help reduce exposure to climate‑related extreme events:

  • Improving early‑warning systems so families know when danger is coming.
  • Supporting stronger housing that can withstand storms and heat.
  • Creating community emergency plans with safe routes and shelters.
  • Providing education on disaster preparedness and safety.
  • Strengthening access to clean water and food during emergencies.

These steps help protect lives and reduce long‑term poverty caused by disasters.

Where You Can Donate

Donations help provide emergency supplies, rebuild communities, and support long‑term resilience. These organizations focus on poverty reduction and disaster protection:

  • UNICEF — supports children and families in crisis.
  • Red Cross / Red Crescent — provides emergency response and disaster relief.
  • Oxfam — works to reduce poverty and strengthen climate resilience.
  • Save the Children — protects vulnerable children during disasters.
  • Local food banks — directly support families in your area.

Even small contributions help protect vulnerable communities from future risks.
