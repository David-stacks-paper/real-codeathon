Understanding SDG Target 1.5

SDG Target 1.5 focuses on strengthening the resilience of vulnerable populations and reducing their exposure to climate‑related extreme events, economic shocks, and social hardships. Communities facing poverty are often the most affected by disasters, making resilience a key part of ending poverty in all its forms.

Building Resilience by 2030

Target 1.5 aims to ensure that by 2030, all countries—especially developing regions—build stronger systems that protect people from disasters and long‑term risks. This includes improving early‑warning systems, investing in climate‑resilient infrastructure, and supporting communities that are most at risk.

When disasters strike, families living in poverty often lose their homes, income, and access to essential services. By strengthening resilience, governments and communities can reduce these impacts and help people recover faster. This target highlights the importance of preparedness, sustainable development, and protecting human lives from preventable harm.

Why Resilience Matters

Building resilience helps break the cycle of poverty. When communities have strong infrastructure, emergency plans, and social support systems, they are better able to withstand crises and rebuild their lives. This is essential for achieving long‑term progress toward ending poverty worldwide.
